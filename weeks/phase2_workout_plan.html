RSW Phase 2 — Build & Burn

Phase: Build & Burn — 6-week hypertrophy & metabolic conditioning • Weeks 7–12
Goal of week 7: Establish your new baseline. Focus on feeling the muscle — slow down your tempo, especially on the way down. Every rep should be intentional. This week is about building the mind-muscle connection that will drive your results over the next 6 weeks.

Welcome to Phase 2 — this is where the real transformation begins. You've built your foundation over the last 6 weeks, and now it's time to put that strength to work. Three focused sessions a week is all you need when you train with intention and purpose.

Goal of week 8: Add one set to each compound movement this week. You're moving from 3 sets to 4 sets on your big lifts. Keep the weight the same as Week 7 — this is a volume week, not a load week. Your muscles need to adapt to more total work before they can handle more weight.

Week 8 is where consistency starts to pay off. You might not see it in the mirror yet, but your muscles are changing at the cellular level. Stay the course. Three sessions, full effort, quality over quantity.

Goal of week 9: Load week. This week you increase weight on every compound lift by 5–10 lbs while dropping reps slightly to 10-12. You're shifting from volume adaptation to load adaptation. This is where strength and muscle are built simultaneously.

You've earned this week. Two weeks of building volume means your body is ready to handle more load. Pick up something heavier than you think you can — then do it with perfect form. That's the whole game.

Goal of week 10: Density week. Same weights as Week 9, but rest periods shorten by 15 seconds on all supersets. Less rest = more metabolic demand = more fat burning while preserving muscle. You will feel this one.

This is the week that separates those who show up from those who show out. Your weights stay the same but your rest drops — it will feel harder, and that's exactly the point. Three sessions. Full intensity. You've got this.

Goal of week 11: Peak week. This is your heaviest and most demanding week of the entire 12-week program. You'll push to near-maximal loads on all compound lifts with 8 reps. This week will show you how strong you've actually become. Leave nothing in the tank.

This is the week you've been building toward for 10 weeks. You are stronger than you were in September. You are stronger than you were a month ago. Pick up the heavy thing. You've earned it.

Goal of week 12: Deload week. Drop to 60% of your Week 11 load, reduce sets to 2-3, and let your body absorb everything you've built. This is not a rest week — it's a strategic recovery week. The adaptations from the last 11 weeks solidify during this week. Do not skip it.

The deload is not optional — it's the point. Your muscles grow during recovery, not during training. This week is the most important investment you can make for your next cycle. Pull back so you can come back stronger. You've done the hard work. Now let it work on you.
